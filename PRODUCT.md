# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, tech leads, and engineering directors at software companies in Taiwan. They see many candidates; the slides are shown in a live interview or shared asynchronously. Audience reads Traditional Chinese comfortably but also understands English technical terms.

## Product Purpose

A personal interview presentation deck for Josh Wang — a software developer with 10+ years of web/mobile architecture experience. The slides replace a generic resume and demonstrate architectural thinking, delivery record, and AI-assisted workflow through concrete evidence. Success looks like the interviewer walking away with a clear, differentiated mental model of who Josh is and what he delivers.

## Brand Personality

Precise, architectural, delivery-confident. Quiet confidence — not self-promotional. Shows rather than tells.

## Anti-references

- Flashy startup pitch decks: excessive motion, big vanity metrics, VC-speak aesthetic, over-animated transitions.
- Generic resume dump: bullet-point walls, no visual hierarchy, CV-pasted-into-slides style.
- Slides that look indistinguishable from an AI-generated dark portfolio template.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence over assertion** — every claim is backed by a concrete outcome or artefact.
2. **Architectural calm** — the visual system should feel like it was designed with intent, not assembled from defaults.
3. **Bilingual first** — Traditional Chinese leads; English technical terms are kept only when they carry more precision than their Chinese equivalents.
4. **Legibility at presentation scale** — text must be readable at arm's length on a projected screen and on a small phone screen; small text is only acceptable when it is truly secondary.
5. **Restrained motion** — transitions exist to orient, not entertain.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Slides must be usable on mobile (shared link fallback). Reduced motion respected. Chinese text must break at meaningful phrase boundaries.
